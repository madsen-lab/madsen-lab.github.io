Projects

Interpretable ML

Machine learning is a powerful tool to fit models to complex biological mechanisms. However, these models are traditionally blackboxes, which limits their usuability to prediction tasks. Emerging methods attempt to overcome this limitation by introducting the concept of interpretable machine learning, where the internal modal can be deciphered. By applying these novel machine learning technologies, we aim to explore complex biological mechanisms.

Metabolic disease

Acquired metabolic diseases, such as obesity and type 2 diabetes, are some of the most prevalent diseases in the world. Their etiology is highly complex and involves multiple tissues. Healthy tissues have the ability to adapt to change in their enviroment, such as changes in nutrient supply - an ability known as tissue plasticity. We study the mechanisms of tissue plasticity in health and in metabolic disease in order to investigate the key regulators of the underlying process.

Transcriptional regulation

The expression levels of genes defines the functional spectrum of any given cell. Thus, in order for a cell to fundamentally change its function, it needs to regulate gene expression. Thus, transcriptional regulation is an essential process for both development and for long-term adaptation. We study the basic mechanisms, elements and factors of transcriptional regulations in order to dissect the rules and logic underlying control of gene expression. 

Selected publications

Plasticity of Epididymal Adipose Tissue in Reponse to Diet-Induced Obesity at Single-Nucleus Resolution

Sárvári and Van Hauwaert et al. (2020): Cell Metabolism

We created an atlas of epididymal white adipose tissue from lean and obese mice at single nucleus resolution and charted it to discover wide-spread transcriptional and compositional reprogramming of cell types and subtypes in obesity. 

Highly interconnected enhancer communities control lineage-determining gens in human mesenchymal stem cells.

Madsen et al. (2020): Nature Genetics

We performed enhancer-capture Hi-C during adipogenesis and osteogenesis of human mesenchymal stem cells and found that enhancers form communities in 3D, which are intimately linked with gene regulation during differentiation. 

Integrated analysis of motif activity and gene expression changes of transcription factors.

Madsen et al. (2018): Genome Research.

We created a novel statistical learning method for prediction of causal transcription factors and benchmarked it against alternative to show that our method outperforms existing methods in finding known factors.
